# SOLARSAFE Materials Team — GM2 2025

## Introduction

SOLARSAFE is a solar-powered maize dryer for smallholder farmers in Kenya and sub-Saharan Africa. Aflatoxin contamination of maize is a serious public health problem: it causes poisoning, liver damage, and stunts child development. The Materials Team evaluated optimal plastic bag materials for the SOLARSAFE drying enclosure.

## Project goal

Find a bag material that: withstands NaOCl cleaning, reduces aflatoxin trapping, retains high internal moisture during drying, is transparent, low cost, and durable.

## Materials evaluated

PVC, PP, and Nylon were shortlisted. LDPE was eliminated based on literature evidence of Aspergillus degradation.

## Key findings

**Experiment 1 (NaOCl ATR-FTIR):** PP and PVC showed no chemical degradation. Nylon gave inconclusive results.

**Experiment 2 (Moisture retention):** PP and Nylon had highest moisture retention. PVC was moderate.

**Recommendation:** PP and PVC are the strongest candidates. Further testing needed for Nylon, PE, and PET.
