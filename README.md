# SOLARSAFE Materials Team — GM2 2025

## Introduction

SOLARSAFE is a solar-powered maize dryer designed for smallholder farmers in Kenya and sub-Saharan Africa. Aflatoxin contamination of maize is a serious public health problem: it can cause poisoning, liver damage, and impaired child development.

The Materials Team evaluated suitable plastic bag materials for the SOLARSAFE drying enclosure.

## Project Goal

Find a bag material that:

* Withstands sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl) cleaning
* Reduces aflatoxin trapping
* Supports effective moisture control during drying
* Is transparent
* Is low cost
* Is durable

## Materials Evaluated

PVC, PP, and Nylon were shortlisted for testing.

LDPE was eliminated based on literature evidence of *Aspergillus* degradation.

## Key Findings

### Experiment 1 — NaOCl ATR-FTIR

PP and PVC showed no clear chemical degradation after sodium hypochlorite exposure.

Nylon gave inconclusive results and requires further testing.

### Experiment 2 — Moisture Retention

PP and Nylon showed the highest moisture retention.

PVC showed moderate moisture retention.

### Recommendation

PVC is the strongest current candidate because it combines chemical stability, transparency, availability, and acceptable moisture performance.

Further testing is needed for Nylon, PE, and PET before making a final material recommendation.
